Title: Data Protection Act 2018 - Plain-Language Overview
Source: UK Public General Acts - Data Protection Act 2018
URL: https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/2018/12/contents
Topic: Privacy, Data Protection, and Information Rights

This is a plain-language educational summary.
It provides high-level information and does not replace official legal guidance.

Purpose:
The Data Protection Act 2018 works with the UK GDPR to regulate processing of personal data in the UK.
It supports privacy rights and accountability when organisations handle personal information.

Core principles typically discussed:
- lawfulness, fairness, and transparency
- purpose limitation
- data minimisation
- accuracy
- storage limitation
- integrity and confidentiality
- accountability

Common individual rights:
- right to be informed
- right of access
- right to rectification
- right to erasure in certain conditions
- right to restrict processing in certain conditions
- right to object in certain contexts

Connection to human rights:
Data protection is closely linked to privacy values often discussed under Article 8 ECHR and the Human Rights
Act context.

Public body and organisational duties:
Organisations must implement governance, security, and lawful processing practices, with additional rules for
sensitive data and law enforcement contexts.
