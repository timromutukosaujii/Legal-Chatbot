Title: Equality Act 2010 - Plain-Language Overview
Source: UK Public General Acts - Equality Act 2010
URL: https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/2010/15/contents
Topic: Equality and Anti-Discrimination Law

This is a plain-language educational summary.
It explains high-level principles in the Equality Act 2010 for informational chatbot use.

Main purpose:
The Equality Act 2010 brings together major UK anti-discrimination rules into one framework.
It protects people from unfair treatment based on protected characteristics.

Protected characteristics include:
- age
- disability
- gender reassignment
- marriage and civil partnership (in specific contexts)
- pregnancy and maternity
- race
- religion or belief
- sex
- sexual orientation

Common legal concepts:
- direct discrimination
- indirect discrimination
- harassment
- victimisation

Where protections apply:
- work and recruitment
- education
- services and public functions
- housing in many contexts
- associations

Public sector equality duty:
Certain public bodies must have due regard to equality aims, including reducing disadvantage and fostering
good relations.

Reasonable adjustments:
Employers and service providers may have duties to make reasonable adjustments for disabled people so they
are not substantially disadvantaged.

Scope note:
This file is informational and not case-specific legal advice.
