Title: European Convention on Human Rights (ECHR) - Plain-Language Overview
Source: Council of Europe - European Convention on Human Rights
URL: https://www.echr.coe.int/
Topic: International Human Rights Framework

This file gives a plain-language overview of the ECHR for educational RAG use.
The ECHR is an international treaty. UK law also reflects these rights through the Human Rights Act 1998.

Purpose:
The ECHR protects fundamental civil and political rights across member states of the Council of Europe.
Individuals can apply to the European Court of Human Rights after exhausting domestic remedies.

Key rights commonly referenced in UK legal discussion:
- Article 2: right to life
- Article 3: prohibition of torture and inhuman or degrading treatment
- Article 5: liberty and security
- Article 6: fair hearing by an independent tribunal
- Article 8: private and family life, home and correspondence
- Article 9: freedom of religion and belief
- Article 10: freedom of expression
- Article 11: assembly and association
- Article 14: non-discrimination in enjoyment of Convention rights

How balancing works:
- Some rights are absolute and cannot be restricted (for example Article 3).
- Some rights are qualified and can be limited by law if restrictions are necessary and proportionate.
- Courts often apply a proportionality analysis when rights conflict.

Why it matters in UK legal information:
The ECHR framework shapes UK public law, especially in human-rights-sensitive issues involving state power,
privacy, free expression, policing, detention, and fair process.
