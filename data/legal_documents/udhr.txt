Title: Universal Declaration of Human Rights (UDHR) - Plain-Language Overview
Source: United Nations - UDHR
URL: https://www.un.org/en/about-us/universal-declaration-of-human-rights
Topic: Foundational Human Rights Principles

The UDHR (1948) is a foundational international human-rights statement.
It is not a UK statute, but it strongly influenced later human-rights instruments and legal thinking.

Common principles include:
- dignity and equality of all persons,
- freedom from torture and degrading treatment,
- fair hearing rights,
- privacy and family life values,
- freedom of thought, belief, and expression,
- social and economic protections in broad terms.

Educational role in this dataset:
The UDHR helps provide conceptual background for understanding modern rights frameworks such as the ECHR and
rights discourse in UK public law.

Use note:
For UK legal application, users should also consult UK legislation and official guidance.
