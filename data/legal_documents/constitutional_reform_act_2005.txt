Title: Constitutional Reform Act 2005 - Plain-Language Overview
Source: UK Public General Acts - Constitutional Reform Act 2005
URL: https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/2005/4/contents
Topic: UK Constitution and Separation of Powers

This document summarizes key constitutional themes for educational RAG use.

The Constitutional Reform Act 2005 is important because it reshaped institutional arrangements in the UK,
including judicial independence and the role of top courts.

Key themes:
- Separation of powers: it clarifies distinctions between judiciary, executive, and legislature.
- Judicial independence: public authorities, including ministers, must uphold judicial independence.
- UK Supreme Court: the Act created the Supreme Court of the United Kingdom, replacing the appellate role
  of the House of Lords.

Why it matters:
The Act is central to modern constitutional structure and to understanding rule-of-law safeguards in the UK.
It is often discussed alongside human rights and constitutional accountability.

Practical relevance:
Questions about constitutional roles, legal checks on executive power, and court independence often connect
to principles reflected in this Act.
