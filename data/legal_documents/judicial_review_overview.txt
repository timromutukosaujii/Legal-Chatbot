Judicial review in the UK — Overview (general information)

Summary (general information)
- Judicial review is a court process used to challenge whether a public body has acted lawfully.
- It is generally about the legality of decision-making, not simply whether a decision was right or wrong on its merits.
- Common grounds include illegality, procedural unfairness, and irrationality.
- Applications must usually be made promptly and are handled in specialist court procedures.
